It's not just Coffee
It's Starbucks

The history of coffee is rich with legend and intrigue. It all began long ago, circa AD 800–900, with an Ethiopian goat herder, Kaldi, who noticed his goats became energetic after eating berries of a certain shrub.

A monk who came across Kaldi crushed them into a powder and added boiling water. This may have been the first-ever cup of coffee. Upon realizing the concoction released energy, he rushed back to his monastery to share his findings which the monks as a “gift from the Lord”.

Circa 1000, traders brought coffee across the Red Sea into Yemen (Arabia), where the beans were first roasted and the plant first cultivated. As Islam spread to North Africa, the Mediterranean, and Asia, coffee traveled along. In 1300 Coffee Traveled with the spread of Islam. From 1450–1650, coffeehouses appeared throughout the Arabian world and coffee began being used for medicinal purposes.

From 1615–1700 Coffee enters Europe through the Port of Venice. A Venetian merchant fell in love with coffee in Turkey. So enraptured was he that he brought it back to sell in Italy. Soon coffee houses opened in Italy (1645–1655), London (1652), and Paris (1672). Many important institutions originated in coffeehouses—stockbrokers gathered at Garraway’s in London evolved into the London Stock Exchange.
